Overview

The SWIFT Project (Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication) is the backbone of the global financial system. Contrary to popular belief, SWIFT does not move money; it moves information. It provides a secure, standardized network for financial institutions to send and receive information about financial transactions.

Established in 1973, it now connects more than 11,000 banking organizations and corporations in over 200 countries.

SWIFT gpi (Global Payments Innovation)

One of the most significant recent projects is SWIFT gpi. This initiative was launched to address the historical pain points of cross-border payments: speed, transparency, and end-to-end tracking.

  • Speed: Nearly 50% of gpi payments are credited within 30 minutes.
  • Tracking: Banks can track their payments in real-time, much like a parcel delivery.
  • Certainty: Full transparency on fees and exchange rates.

The ISO 20022 Migration

The financial world is currently undergoing a massive "project" of migrating to the ISO 20022 standard. This is a multi-year transition to a highly structured and data-rich messaging format.

This allows for better automation, improved compliance checking, and fewer manual interventions, making the global economy more efficient.

The Future: CBDCs and Blockchain

The SWIFT Project is currently exploring the integration of Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) and tokenized assets. By experimenting with blockchain interoperability, SWIFT aims to ensure that the existing financial infrastructure can communicate with the "digital currencies of tomorrow."
